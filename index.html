About me

I am a recent graduate of the University of Texas Rio Grande Valley with a Master's of Science in Physics. My master's thesis project consisted in the theoretical study of the dynamical Casimir effect in a newly proposed superconducting circuit architecture where fast oscillations in boundary conditions lead to the excitation of quantum vacuum states into real photon pairs. Our system has the potential to provide a tunable source of non-classical microwave photons for superconducting quantum technologies. Publication of this work is currently underway.

I am currently open to professional as well as PhD position opportunities. I am looking for an active and collaborative environment to pour myself into work with projects related to quantum information technologies.

My research interests include relativistic quantum effects and their applications to quantum information science, the use of quantum systems to accurately and precisely measure time in quantum metrology, and the characterization of noise and coherence in open quantum systems. I have some years of experience programming for scientific research applications. Proficient in mathematical and computational modeling of physical systems, as well as data analysis and visualization.

Other interests include music composition and performance (guitar, vocals, and percussion), cycling and bicycle touring, language learning and exchange, as well as cooking, literature, and philosophy.
